Abstract
In the following paper, a proof founded upon number-theoretic results will be exposed for
the theorem that, among the natural numbers of the form 1111. . . 1, that is, constructed of
n ones juxtaposed—for a given integer n greater than zero—, 1 is the sole element of such
succession which veriﬁes the property of being a perfect square, namely the square of another
positive integer.
